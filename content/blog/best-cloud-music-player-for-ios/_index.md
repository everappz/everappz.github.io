## What Is the Best Cloud Music Player for iPhone?

The best cloud music player for iPhone is one that connects directly to your personal cloud storage accounts, lets you stream or download tracks for offline listening, and gives you full control over your library without a monthly subscription tied to a specific catalog. Evermusic meets all of these criteria. It integrates with Dropbox, Google Drive, OneDrive, Box, MEGA, Yandex.Disk, pCloud, WebDAV, SMB, and several other services -- over 12 cloud platforms in total. Unlike mainstream streaming apps that lock you into their catalogs, Evermusic plays the files you already own, stored wherever you choose. The app supports a wide range of audio formats including MP3, FLAC, AAC, M4A, WAV, AIFF, and OGG, so virtually any music file in your cloud storage is playable without conversion.

## Why Does Evermusic Stand Out?

Evermusic stands out because it treats your cloud storage as a true music library rather than just a file browser. Once you connect a cloud account, the app automatically scans and indexes your audio files, reads embedded metadata and album artwork, and organizes everything into a familiar library view with artists, albums, genres, and playlists. It includes a built-in ID3 tag editor so you can correct track titles, artist names, and album information directly on your device. The equalizer offers multiple presets along with manual band adjustment, and the audio engine supports gapless playback and crossfade for uninterrupted listening sessions. Background playback, Lock Screen controls, and full integration with CarPlay and AirPlay mean Evermusic works seamlessly across the Apple ecosystem without requiring any additional configuration from the user.

## What Cloud Services Does Evermusic Support?

Evermusic supports connectivity with more than 12 cloud storage platforms, making it the most versatile cloud music player available on iOS. The supported services include Dropbox, Google Drive, OneDrive, Box, MEGA, Yandex.Disk, pCloud, Backblaze B2, Cloudflare R2, and any server running WebDAV or SMB protocols.
