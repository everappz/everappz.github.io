## What Is Lossless Audio?

Lossless audio preserves every detail from the original studio recording. Unlike MP3 and AAC, which discard parts of the audio signal to reduce file size, lossless compression retains the complete waveform. The decoded output is bit-for-bit identical to the source.

Key differences between lossless and lossy audio:

- **Dynamic range**: Lossless preserves the full range from quiet to loud passages
- **Stereo imaging**: Spatial positioning of instruments remains accurate
- **Detail**: Subtle textures in acoustic instruments, vocals, and orchestral recordings are retained

Common lossless formats and their resolutions:

- **FLAC / ALAC**: CD-quality at 16-bit/44.1 kHz, hi-res up to 24-bit/192 kHz and beyond
- **DSD**: Uses extremely high sample rates (DSD64, DSD128, DSD256), originally developed for Super Audio CD
- **WAV / AIFF**: Uncompressed lossless audio

## Tips for Better Lossless Playback

- **Use a dedicated DAC**: USB-C and Lightning DAC adapters bypass your device's built-in converter for better output quality
- **Use wired headphones**: Bluetooth compresses audio, reducing the benefit of lossless files
- **Adjust the equalizer**: Tune the 10-band EQ to match your headphones or speakers
- **Organize metadata**: Accurate tags and consistent folder structures make large libraries easier to navigate
- **Choose hi-res files**: 24-bit/96 kHz or higher provides maximum detail
- **Stable network for NAS streaming**: Use wired Ethernet or strong Wi-Fi to avoid buffering with large DSD or hi-res FLAC files
